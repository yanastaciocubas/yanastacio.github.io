My story

I’m Yonara Anastacio, a first‑generation student from Lima, Peru, studying Computer Science and Public Health at Columbia University. I grew up in a fishing village, and now build creative projects that tries to bridge the gap between rigorous computation and real human needs.

My work sits at the intersection of research and engineering: I care deeply about accessible interfaces so others can reuse scientific work. I teach and mentor students from underrepresented backgrounds and believe technology should be understandable and useful.

When I’m not debugging simulation seeds or writing tests, I compose music inspired by quantum systems, explore data visualizations, and draw (often on Procreate)bringing ideas from sketch to deployed product.
