I'm a first-generation computer scientist from Lima, Peru. I double-concentrated in Computer Science and Public Health at Columbia, which sounds unusual until you realize most public health problems are, at their core, data infrastructure problems.

At Columbia's Data Science Institute I spent two years building open-access curriculum for the Northeast Big Data Innovation Hub — five notebooks covering XGBoost, quantum computing, and algorithmic bias, used by students across the region.

I'm bilingual in English and Spanish and I can speak French. I'm a TED translator and a Salesforce Agentblazer Champion.
